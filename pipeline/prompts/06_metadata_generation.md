# Metadata generation (§6)

Used by `pipeline.generate_metadata()`.
Substitutes: `{NICHE_STYLE_GUIDE}`, `{SCRIPT}`.

Reference: `prompts/library/thumbnail_patterns.md` (the 8 named cover-frame patterns the renderer knows how to draw).

```
Generate publishing metadata for this video.

NICHE & STYLE GUIDE:
{NICHE_STYLE_GUIDE}

SCRIPT:
{SCRIPT}

Audience pivot (HARD RULE — set 2026-05-07 evening): the audience is a **general consumer curious about AI, not a developer**. Every visible field below (especially the title and the first three hashtags) must be understandable to a non-coder in 2 seconds. Reject any draft that requires dev jargon (CLI, refactor, MCP, lint, repo, env var, etc.) to land. Replace dev-jargon with plain-English equivalents OR pivot the framing.

Produce:

YOUTUBE SHORTS:
- Title (max 60 chars. **No hashtags in the visible title** — hashtags go in the description only. **Format preference (set 2026-05-18 cycle-11 feedback, reinforced 2026-05-20 cluster audit):** PREFER a **single-sentence narrative title** that leads with the named subject and tells a mini-story. Examples that hit: "Elon Musk just lost his biggest AI fight", "Claude AI pretended to be the internet", "Jamie Dimon: Claude built my dashboard in 20 minutes". Use **colon-template** (`[Subject]: [payoff]`) ONLY when the subject and payoff naturally split AND the narrative version would feel forced — colon is the EXCEPTION, not the default. In either format: specific, not clickbait, includes one searchable keyword. **Plain-English check: a regular ChatGPT-using consumer must grasp the topic in 2 seconds.**
  - **Recognizable anchor in the FIRST 3 WORDS (set 2026-06-07, sharpened 2026-06-11 title audit: top-decile titles are 6/6 recognizable-anchor-led; generic-first-word titles run median 182v vs 261v entity-led).** The title's first 3 words MUST contain a recognizable proper noun: a mainstream AI brand (Claude, ChatGPT, OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, Google, Apple, Siri, Microsoft, Grok, Meta), a household-name human (Musk, Altman — not an AI-Twitter-only name), or a universal consumer object (iPhone, your phone, your inbox). **BANNED as the opening:** a determiner + generic-AI noun — "This AI...", "Your AI...", "A new AI...", "Hackers...", "Researchers...". If the only human is obscure (an unknown engineer/researcher/user a general viewer wouldn't know), DROP them from the title and lead with the AI brand + a motion verb instead ("Claude quietly...", "ChatGPT just...", "Gemini can now..."). Recognizability beats accuracy-of-attribution: a famous name or mainstream AI brand earns the click; an unknown name or "This AI" wastes the highest-value real estate in the title.
  - **Title length 48–58 characters (set 2026-06-07, breakout analysis: 50–58-char titles pull ~2x the 40–50-char bucket; sub-40-char titles under-reach because they're vague, not because they're short).** Use the room for a named subject PLUS a specific payoff. The mechanism is specificity, not length itself — do not pad to hit the count; earn the characters with concrete detail. Stay under 60 to avoid YouTube's mobile-truncation cutoff.
  - **No journalistic-byline colon when the human is only the SOURCE, not the surprise (set 2026-06-07, breakout analysis + style-guide directive: `[Unknown Name]: [payoff]` reads boring and under-reaches when the named person is merely who reported the thing).** If the human is just the source of the observation, drop them from the title and lead with the AI in a catchy narrative sentence instead. Reserve the colon shape for when the human IS the payoff / the surprise (e.g., "ChatGPT 5.5 Pro shocked a Fields medalist", "...stunned a Nobel laureate"). **"Recognizable" means household-name or prestige-status, NOT AI-famous.** Counts: Elon Musk, Sam Altman, "a Fields medalist", "a Nobel laureate", a named billion-dollar firm (Figma, Jane Street). Does NOT count (drop from title, lead with the brand): individual engineers/researchers known only inside AI — past misses that under-reached: "Luke Lanchester" (207v), "Tom Brown" (245v), "Andrej Karpathy" (115v), "DeepMind scientist Alex Turner" (113v). When unsure whether a name is famous enough, assume it is NOT and lead with the AI brand.
  - **HARD RULE — modal-framing ban in the title (set 2026-06-09 review, R2 H2: the 10-view-floor autopsy — hypothetical titles tank reach).** The title must state a DATED FACTUAL EVENT — something that actually happened. Modal / hypothetical openers are BANNED in the title: "could", "might", "imagine if", "what if". "Claude just rewrote its own code" ships; "What if AI rewrote its own code?" does not. The same ban applies to the script's first sentence at Stage 1.5 (`script_quality.modal_ban_enabled`).
  - **Durable rule, restated (set 2026-05-18 cycle-11 feedback; restated 2026-06-09 review per Manager C4):** the title stays a **single-sentence narrative** ("AI quietly built itself a parallel internet"), NOT the `[Name]: [payoff]` colon template. "Dated factual event" does not license a regression to the colon shape — state the event as a narrative sentence; the colon remains the rare exception per the rules above.)
  - **Withhold the twist on FEATURE topics (set 2026-06-11 title audit).** When the topic is a capability announcement ("X now does Y"), do NOT write a flat, fully-resolved title that states the whole proposition — a viewer who learns everything from the title has no reason to watch ("Apple's camera AI now narrates who's at your door" tells you the entire feature). Surface the **unsettling implication or the stakes**, not just the capability: lead with the surprise, the catch, or the "wait, that means..." angle and leave the mechanism for the video. A good title makes a promise it does not keep in the title.
- Description (3–5 sentences, no URLs. The channel is too new to embed clickable links in descriptions — YouTube blocks them until the channel hits the verification threshold. Include: hook line, 1-sentence summary, AI disclosure line. Source citation is fine as a plain-text reference (e.g., "Source: Bloomberg via 9to5Mac") but no URLs. Mention sources by NAME, not URL. End the description body BEFORE the hashtag block.)
- Tags: 10 (commas-separated; mix of consumer-AI buzz words and topic-specific terms — e.g., for an Apple-iOS-AI video: `apple, ios 27, ai, chatgpt, claude, gemini, iphone ai, ai news, anthropic, openai`)
- Hashtags: **10–12 hashtags** at the bottom of the description (since titles are hashtag-free, the description is the only discovery surface for hashtags). The first one must be `#Shorts`. The next 2 must be the most search-relevant consumer-AI tags for the topic — these surface above the title as clickable chips. Then 7–9 more consumer-AI buzz words. Use a mix from this canonical list per video: `#Shorts #AI #ChatGPT #Claude #Gemini #OpenAI #Anthropic #AInews #AItools #AIagents #ArtificialIntelligence #FutureTech #TechNews #GoogleAI #AppleIntelligence #iOS27` (only relevant ones — don't pad with off-topic). **NEVER** use dev-only tags (`#DevTools` `#Python` `#PromptEngineering`). NEVER exceed 15 hashtags total — YouTube ignores ALL hashtags above 15. Output the YouTube hashtag block exactly ONCE, inside the description (after the AI-disclosure line). Do NOT also emit a separate labeled "Hashtags:" line in the YouTube section — that block belongs only to TikTok and Instagram.

TIKTOK:
- Caption (max 150 chars, conversational, 1 hook line + CTA, plain English)
- 4 hashtags (mix of broad and niche, consumer-facing — `#TechTok`, `#AI`, `#ChatGPT`, etc.; not dev-only)

INSTAGRAM REELS:
- Caption (3–5 sentences, can be longer than TikTok, slightly more polished tone, plain English)
- 5 hashtags (consumer-facing AI mix)

COVER / THUMBNAIL CONCEPT:
- Pattern: pick ONE from the named patterns in prompts/library/thumbnail_patterns.md. Output the exact name in this list:
    big_text_claim | crossed_out | terminal_with_result | comparison_frame | big_number | tool_logo_spotlight | question_hook | before_after_stack
  Default to `big_text_claim` when in doubt — it's the channel's house pattern. Note: `terminal_with_result` is dev-coded and should be avoided for general-audience pivot videos unless the script genuinely centers on a coding moment a non-coder would still find interesting.
- Text overlay: 1–3 words, ALL CAPS, the punchiest distillation of the script's claim — **plain English, no dev jargon** (e.g., "AI WROTE THIS.", "10 SECONDS LATER...", "WAIT — IT DID WHAT?"). For `big_number`, the text is the number itself plus a 1-2 word context label. For `crossed_out`, output two short labels separated by ` -> ` (e.g., "old AI -> new AI").
  - **Cover IS the first video frame on Shorts and cannot be changed after publish (set 2026-06-07, breakout analysis: the Shorts cover doubles as frame one, so it carries first-impression reach with zero do-over).** Treat the overlay budget as up to 3–5 words if the claim needs them (keep brand constants locked), pair it with the SINGLE most striking image the script implies, and make the named subject (the famous human or the AI brand) VISIBLE in the frame whenever the title names one — the cover and title should reinforce the same recognizable subject. **The overlay must COMPLEMENT the title, not repeat its words (set 2026-06-11 title audit).** Cover + title are two surfaces — together they should complete the hook, not say the same thing twice. Prefer the concrete payoff the title withholds: a hard number ("183 CRIMES", "2 CENTS"), a reaction, or the stakes word. If the title already contains your overlay phrase, pick a different angle (the number, the consequence, the "wait" beat).
- Background image description: one sentence describing what fills the frame around the text. Used as guidance only when the renderer falls back to stock-footage frames; the locked patterns in thumbnail_patterns.md don't currently consume it.
- Color accent (HEX): always `#7C5CFF` (channel violet). Locked.

PINNED COMMENT:
- ONE short line (no URL, ≤140 chars) in the friend-retelling voice, posing the SAME stakes-tied closing question the script ends on. This is what the operator pastes and pins as the channel's own first comment at upload — it seeds the comment thread that Shorts weights heavily. Make it genuinely answerable and specific to THIS video's twist (e.g., "Okay be honest — would you let it run your inbox? Yes or no?"). NOT a keyword-bait ("comment X for the link"), NOT a generic "what do you think?", NO link of any kind (the channel can't ship clickable links yet). One line only.

Output each section clearly labeled. No emoji unless the niche style guide explicitly allows them. The PINNED COMMENT section is optional-but-preferred; if you emit it, label it exactly `PINNED COMMENT:` so it is parsed as its own section and never bleeds into the COVER fields.
```
